You are classifying the PRIMARY INTENT of an NIH grant.

Task:
Determine whether this grant is primarily a MECHANISTIC / BASIC SCIENCE project.

Definition of MECHANISTIC INTENT:
The central goal is to understand, explain, or uncover causal biological mechanisms, pathways, or processes. The main scientific contribution is knowledge about how or why a biological system behaves as it does.

Key criteria (must be central, not incidental):
- The primary goal is to understand how or why a biological system behaves as it does. This may include identifying mechanisms, pathways, system dynamics, or biological relationships, even if direct causal manipulation is not performed.
- The main deliverable is biological understanding, not a product or intervention
- The work tests hypotheses about mechanisms, pathways, regulation, or interactions
- Success is defined by explanatory insight, not by performance of a tool or efficacy of a therapy

NOT mechanistic if:
- The goal is to develop, optimize, or evaluate a therapy (drug, vaccine, intervention)
- The goal is to develop or validate a diagnostic or predictive model
- The goal is to build a tool, platform, or method (even if biology is studied)
- The project is purely observational with the primary goal of identifying associations,
risk factors, or population-level correlations WITHOUT aiming to explain biological processes.
- Mechanistic language (e.g., “pathway”, “mechanism”) is used only to support another goal
- Mechanistic studies are only one aim within a larger therapeutic, clinical, or tool-building project
- If the primary goal is to predict response to therapy, classify patients, identify biomarkers for clinical use
- The study primarily links biological variables to outcomes without explaining system-level processes

Critical rule (FINAL):

Ask:
“Is the primary goal to understand how a biological system works?”

Then:
- If YES → MECHANISTIC
Even if:
- measurements are central- correlations are used
- biomarkers are mentioned
Only say NO if:
- the study stops at prediction or classification

Clarification:
- Measurement, observation, or correlation are valid IF used to understand system behavior
- Prediction, classification, or biomarker development are NOT mechanistic

Additional discriminators:
Observational or correlational data CAN support mechanistic studies
if the goal is to understand biological processes or system behavior.

If the study primarily links biological variables to outcomes
(e.g., disease severity, treatment response, behavior)
without explaining how the underlying system operates,

→ NOT mechanistic

BIOMARKER VS MECHANISM (CRITICAL):
If a biological measurement is being studied to understand:
-why it occurs
- how it is generated
- how it relates to underlying biological or physical processes→ MECHANISTIC
If the measurement is used only to:
- predict outcomes
- classify disease-
 serve as a clinical marker→ NOT mechanistic

Strong signals for MECHANISTIC:
- understanding biological processes, pathways, or regulation
- neural, cellular, or molecular system behavior
- linking biological signals to function or phenotype
- identifying how components of a system interact
- explaining variability in biological outcomes
- studies of physiology, signaling, or system dynamics
- perturbation or manipulation of biological systems (genetic, pharmacologic, environmental) to study function

If the study investigates how a molecular or cellular target functions,
even if it is described as a potential therapeutic target,

→ MECHANISTIC

If the study primarily develops or tests that target as a treatment,

→ NOT mechanistic

Priority rule (IMPORTANT):

If the primary output is:
- a treatment → NOT mechanistic
- a tool/platform → NOT mechanistic

even if biological understanding is also generated

BIOMARKER DISAMBIGUATION (REPLACEMENT):
If a biomarker is mentioned, ask:
Is the project trying to USE the biomarker for prediction/classification?
→ YES = NOT mechanistic
OR
Is the project trying to UNDERSTAND how or why the biomarker arises?
→ YES = MECHANISTIC

MEASUREMENT-DRIVEN MECHANISM (CRITICAL):
If a study:
- measures a biological or physical property, AND
- aims to understand how that property arises or relates to system behavior,
→ MECHANISTIC
Even if:
- the measurement could later be used as a biomarker
