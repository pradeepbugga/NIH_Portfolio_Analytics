You are classifying the PRIMARY INTENT of an NIH grant.

Task:
Determine whether this grant is primarily DIAGNOSTIC.

Definition of DIAGNOSTIC:

The central goal is to develop, validate, or apply a method, model, or assay to DETECT, CLASSIFY, STRATIFY, or PREDICT outcomes, states, or responses in individuals (patients, participants, or populations).

This includes:
- disease detection or diagnosis
- risk prediction or prognosis
- treatment response prediction
- patient stratification or subtyping
- decision support for clinical or behavioral outcomes

The key feature is that the main output is a predictive or classification system.

Key criteria (must be central, not incidental):
- Builds or validates a test, biomarker panel, algorithm, or model for disease detection/classification
- Evaluates performance (e.g., sensitivity, specificity, predictive accuracy)
- Intended for clinical or near-clinical use (screening, diagnosis, risk stratification)

NOT diagnostic if the primary goal is:
- Studying biological mechanisms (mechanistic)
- Identifying associations or risk factors in populations without building a diagnostic tool (observational epidemiology)
- Developing general-purpose tools, assays, or platforms without disease classification as the main goal (research tool)
- Testing treatments or interventions (therapeutic)
- The study identifies associations or correlations WITHOUT building a predictive model
- The goal is to understand biology rather than predict outcomes
- A model or algorithm is developed but is a general-purpose method (not tied to prediction/classification of a condition)

PRIORITY RULE (MANDATORY):

If a project includes BOTH:
- biological investigation (mechanisms, pathways, biomarkers)
AND
- prediction, classification, or decision support

→ classify as DIAGNOSTIC

UNLESS prediction is clearly secondary.

Rationale:
Prediction/classification defines the primary product.

BIOMARKER RULE:

If biomarkers are used to:
- predict, classify, or stratify → DIAGNOSTIC

If biomarkers are used to:
- understand biological processes → NOT diagnostic

INTERVENTION OVERRIDE:

If the primary goal is to test or evaluate a treatment or intervention,
→ NOT diagnostic

Even if prediction or biomarkers are included.

CLARIFICATION:

Diagnostic projects are NOT limited to clinical assays.

They include:
- computational prediction models
- behavioral or physiological state detection
- epidemiologic or environmental risk prediction
- decision-support systems

Even if:
- machine learning is used
- biology is studied
- multiple data types are integrated

IMPLICIT PREDICTION RULE (CRITICAL):

If a study:
- identifies biomarkers or features AND
- compares groups (e.g., disease vs control, responder vs non-responder) AND
- aims to distinguish, stratify, or explain differences between them

→ classify as DIAGNOSTIC

Even if:
- no explicit model or classifier is built
- no performance metrics are mentioned
