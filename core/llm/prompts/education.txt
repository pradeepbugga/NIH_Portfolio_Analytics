You are classifying the PRIMARY INTENT of an NIH grant.

Task:
Determine whether this grant is primarily an EDUCATION / TRAINING project.

Definition of EDUCATION / TRAINING:
The central goal is to develop, train, or mentor individuals (students, trainees, fellows, or faculty) to become researchers or professionals, typically through structured programs, curricula, mentorship, or career development pathways.

Key criteria (must be central, not incidental):
- The primary deliverable is trained individuals, not data, discoveries, or infrastructure
- The grant includes structured training components (e.g., curriculum, mentorship, fellowships, career development plans)
- Success is defined by trainee outcomes (e.g., publications, career progression, grant funding, workforce development)
- The program explicitly aims to build a pipeline of researchers or professionals

NOT education/training if:
- Training is secondary to another primary goal (e.g., infrastructure, research, tool development)
- The project primarily builds or supports cores, shared resources, or institutional capacity (even if training is included)
- The main goal is to conduct research, even if trainees are involved (e.g., K awards, mentored research projects)
- The project is a conference, workshop, or outreach activity focused on dissemination or networking rather than sustained training
- The project provides services, access, or support rather than developing trainees
- The project funds pilot research or infrastructure with some training components (e.g., RIMI, INBRE, CTSA cores)

Critical rule (VERY IMPORTANT):

If the answer depends on this question:
“Are people being trained as the main product, or is training just a component of something else?”

Then:

- If trained individuals = product → YES
- If training = supporting activity → NO

Edge case guidance (based on common failure modes):
- T32, T34, R25, K12, institutional training programs → YES (training is the product)
- Institutional K-type or CDP-style programs (e.g., K12, SPORE CDP) → YES if they function as structured training pipelines
- CTSA, INBRE, RIMI, U54 partnerships → NO (infrastructure + capacity building, even if heavy training components exist)
- Public education / outreach (SEPA, museum programs) → NO (education ≠ researcher training)

WORKSHOP / SEMINAR DISAMBIGUATION:

- One-off events (R13) → NO
- Recurring structured programs with training goals → YES

PUBLIC VS RESEARCH TRAINING:

- Training researchers / scientists → EDUCATION
- Training general public / K–12 → NOT education (for this taxonomy)

K AWARD DISAMBIGUATION (CRITICAL):

If the grant is a K award:

Ask:
“Is the primary goal to support the development of the investigator through training, mentorship, and career progression?”

- If YES → EDUCATION / TRAINING
- If the grant is primarily a research project with minimal training emphasis → NO

TRAINING VIA RESEARCH (IMPORTANT):

A project CAN be education/training even if it includes research,
IF:
- the research is a vehicle for training
- trainee development is the primary objective

Do NOT classify as research if:
- the main goal is to develop people, not generate knowledge

TRAINING PROGRAMS IN CORES:

If the project:
- provides mentoring, workshops, career development programs
- builds trainee skills or research workforce capacity
- runs structured programs for trainees or junior investigators

→ EDUCATION / TRAINING

Even if:
- it is part of a larger center or infrastructure grant

PRIMARY OUTPUT OVERRIDE (FINAL):

Ask:
“What is being produced at the end of this grant?”

- Trained people → EDUCATION
- New knowledge → MECHANISTIC
- Intervention → THERAPEUTIC
- Model/tool → RESEARCH TOOL
- Prediction/biomarker → DIAGNOSTIC
- Coordination/support → INFRASTRUCTURE

This rule overrides all others.
