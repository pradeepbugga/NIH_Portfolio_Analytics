You are classifying the PRIMARY INTENT of an NIH grant.

Task:
Determine whether this grant is primarily a RESEARCH TOOL / METHOD DEVELOPMENT project.

Definition of RESEARCH TOOL:
The central goal is to create, generalize, or validate a reusable method, platform, assay, device, or computational tool that other researchers could adopt.

The tool may be developed in a specific biological context, but must provide reusable capabilities beyond a single experiment or dataset.

Key criteria (must be central, not incidental):
- The main deliverable is the method/platform itself
- The tool provides reusable capabilities that other researchers working on similar problems could adopt
- Success is defined by usability, scalability, or adoption beyond a single biological application
- The tool can be developed within a specific biological system as long as it provides a reusable experimental or computational capability.

NOT research tool if:
- The method is only used to answer a specific biological question and is not positioned as a reusable capability
- The platform is incidental and not a central deliverable
- The goal is to improve a therapeutic strategy (e.g., drug delivery, gene therapy)
- The work only applies an existing method to a specific system without developing new or improved capabilities
- The primary goal is to develop diagnostic assays or clinical tests, even if they involve method development
- The method is a means, not the primary scientific contribution
-  The project is a core facility, shared resource, or service center
 - The primary goal is to provide access, training, or support for existing methods
 - The work enables other researchers rather than producing a new method itself


Critical rule (VERY IMPORTANT):
If the answer depends on this question:
“Is the method the product, or is it just a means to study or treat something?”
Then:
 If method = product → YES
 If method = means → NO

IMPORTANT CLARIFICATION:

A method/platform is NOT considered “just a means” if:
- it is newly developed or significantly improved, AND
- the proposed experiments depend on it, AND
- it provides reusable capabilities (measurement, modeling, control, or simulation)

Strong signals for RESEARCH TOOL:
- Development of experimental platforms (e.g., microfluidics, organ models, devices)
- Integration of hardware + software + modeling systems
- Creation of computational frameworks, simulations, or ML tools
- Systems designed for measurement, prediction, screening, or control
- Language like: “platform”, “framework”, “tool”, “system”, “model”, “technology”
- Development of new assay systems, engineered biological models, or experimental measurement systems

EDGE CASE RULE (VERY IMPORTANT):

If the project BOTH:
(1) develops a new or significantly improved platform, device, model system, or computational framework, AND
(2) uses it to study biological or clinical questions,

THEN:

Evaluate the role of the platform:

- If the platform is CENTRAL to the project (i.e., a primary deliverable), 
- AND the experiments DEPEND on it (i.e., the science could not proceed without it),
- AND it provides REUSABLE capabilities (measurement, modeling, control, simulation, or screening),

→ YES (research tool)

Otherwise:
- If the platform is incidental, minor, or only supports a specific biological question → NO

IMPORTANT:
Do NOT classify as mechanistic just because biological questions are studied,
but also do NOT classify as a tool unless the platform itself is a primary output.
NEWNESS CHECK (IMPORTANT):

If the project does NOT introduce a new or significantly improved method, platform, or system,
and instead applies existing approaches to a biological question,

→ NOT research tool

Instructions:
- Answer: YES (research tool) or NO (not research tool)
- Provide 2–4 sentences of reasoning that:
- Identify the PRIMARY goal
- Explicitly state whether the method is the product or a means
- Contrast against at least one alternative (therapeutic, mechanistic, etc.)

Be strict:
- Only answer YES if the method/platform is the main output
- If the method is only usable for a narrow, one-off biological question → NO
- If it provides a reusable capability within that domain → YES
- If unsure → answer NO
