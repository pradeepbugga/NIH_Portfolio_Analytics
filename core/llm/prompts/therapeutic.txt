You are classifying the PRIMARY INTENT of an NIH grant.

Task:
Determine whether this grant is primarily a THERAPEUTIC project.

Definition of THERAPEUTIC:
The central goal is to test, evaluate, or demonstrate that an intervention improves health outcomes, disease progression, or clinically relevant endpoints.

Therapeutic interventions are intended to act directly on patients to modify disease biology, even if the current study is preclinical.

This includes all stages of therapeutic development, including:
- discovery and optimization of drug candidates intended for therapeutic use
- (not molecules used only as experimental probes)
- screening, SAR, and lead optimization
- preclinical testing (in vitro or in vivo)
- IND-enabling studies (toxicity, PK, safety)
- clinical trials evaluating efficacy or effectiveness

This also includes BOTH:

1) Therapeutic efficacy studies:
- Directly test improvement in symptoms, disease progression, or survival

2) Therapeutic development studies:
- Develop, optimize, or validate an intervention intended to treat disease
- Includes drug candidates, biologics, delivery systems, or therapeutic devices
- Includes preclinical in vitro or in vivo testing when tied to a treatment goal

Important:
A project can be therapeutic even if clinical outcomes are not yet measured,
as long as the central goal is to create or improve a treatment.

Key criteria (must be central, not incidental):
- An intervention is applied (drug, biologic, behavioral, device, etc.)
- The intervention is the primary focus of the project
Success is defined by either:
  - Demonstrating improvement in clinical or physiological outcomes (efficacy studies), OR
  - Advancing an intervention toward therapeutic use (e.g., optimization, safety, PK/PD, delivery, or preclinical validation)
- The study directly evaluates efficacy, effectiveness, or therapeutic benefit

NOT therapeutic if:
- The goal is to understand biological mechanisms, even if drugs or interventions are used
- The intervention is used as a tool to probe a pathway (e.g., inhibition, knockdown, stimulation)
- The intervention is used only to study biological mechanisms (probe)
- There is no effort to develop or optimize it toward a treatment
- The study measures biomarkers, signaling, imaging, or mechanistic endpoints only
- The project optimizes delivery, adherence, targeting, or implementation of treatments (→ clinical/health systems)
- The project develops diagnostic tools or biomarkers (→ diagnostic)
- The work builds or validates a method, platform, or assay (→ research tool)
- The project is observational, correlational, or epidemiological
- The grant is infrastructure, coordination, or a core facility
- The intervention modifies the environment, infrastructure, or access rather than directly treating individuals
- If the primary goal is to characterize, measure, or understand the effects of an intervention (e.g., biological, physiological, behavioral, or system-level effects), even if those effects are beneficial or clinically relevant.
- compounds, inhibitors, or genetic tools are used only to perturb a system and understand biology, without intent to develop them as treatments

Critical rule (UPDATED):

Ask:
“Is this project trying to build or improve a treatment for disease?”

- If YES → THERAPEUTIC
- If NO → continue evaluation

Then refine:

If the intervention is used:
- to treat disease (even in development stage) → YES
- to study biology/mechanisms → NO


Key disambiguation (VERY IMPORTANT):

Ask:
“Is the intervention itself being developed into a treatment, or just used to study biology?”

- If being developed into a treatment → YES
- If used as a probe to understand mechanisms → NO

Additional disambiguation (IMPORTANT):

Ask:
“Is this intervention specifically tied to treating a disease, or is it a general-purpose technology?”

- If tied to treating a specific disease or condition → YES (THERAPEUTIC)
- If designed as a general platform for research or broad screening → NO (RESEARCH TOOL)

Additional strict rules (from common failure cases):
- A randomized trial is NOT automatically therapeutic — if the primary endpoint is mechanistic (e.g., imaging, signaling), answer NO
- Mention of drugs, inhibitors, or therapies alone is NOT sufficient
- “May lead to therapies” or “therapeutic targets” → NOT therapeutic

Strong signals for THERAPEUTIC:
- “drug-like properties”
- “lead compound” or “lead optimization”
- “SAR (structure-activity relationship)”
- “ADME, PK, PD”
- “IND-enabling studies”
- “clinical development”
- “proof-of-concept in disease models”

If these are central → answer YES, even without human outcomes

Platform / device clarification:

If a platform or device is described, ask:
“Is it intended to be used IN patients to deliver or improve treatment?”

- If YES → THERAPEUTIC
- If NO → likely RESEARCH TOOL
