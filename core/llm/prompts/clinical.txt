You are classifying the PRIMARY INTENT of an NIH grant.

Task:
Determine whether this grant is primarily a CLINICAL / HEALTH SYSTEMS project.

Definition of CLINICAL / HEALTH SYSTEMS:
The central goal is to improve, evaluate, or understand how healthcare is delivered, accessed, or used at the patient, provider, or system level.

This includes:
- Behavioral or psychosocial interventions (e.g., adherence, counseling, lifestyle, decision-making)
- Care delivery models (e.g., workflows, care coordination, telehealth)
- Implementation science (e.g., adoption, feasibility, uptake, dissemination)
- Public health or population-level interventions affecting health behaviors or care access
- Interventions where success depends on changing patient or provider behavior

Key criteria (must be central, not incidental):
- The primary outcome involves patient behavior, care processes, or health service utilization
- The intervention operates through care delivery, behavior change, or system-level mechanisms
- Success is defined by feasibility, adoption, adherence, engagement, or real-world effectiveness
- The study evaluates or modifies how care is delivered—not just what is delivered biologically

NOT clinical / health systems if:
- The primary goal is to test a drug, biologic, or device acting on biology (therapeutic)
- The primary goal is to understand biological mechanisms (mechanistic/basic science)
- The primary goal is to develop or validate a tool, assay, biomarker, or imaging method
- The intervention acts directly on physiology rather than behavior or care delivery
- The study focuses on efficacy under controlled biological conditions rather than real-world care
- The project is observational without testing or evaluating a care or behavioral intervention
- The output informs care but does not change how care is delivered
- Clinical trial networks, consortia, or infrastructure whose purpose is to test interventions rather than change care delivery
- The project primarily conducts or supports clinical trials to evaluate the efficacy, safety, or biological effects of drugs, biologics, devices, or vaccines, even if performed in patients

Critical rule (VERY IMPORTANT):
Ask:“Is the study optimizing how care is delivered, or testing whether an intervention works biologically?”

Then:
- Optimizing delivery/use (behavior, adherence, workflows, access) → YES
- Testing efficacy/safety/biological effect of an intervention → NO

Additional discriminator:
“What must change for the study to succeed?”
- Patient/provider behavior or care processes must change → YES
- Biological markers or physiology must change → NO

Instructions:
- Answer: YES (clinical/health systems) or NO (not)
- Provide 2–4 sentences of reasoning that: 
- Identify the PRIMARY goal 
- Explicitly state whether the intervention acts on behavior/care delivery vs biology 
- Contrast against at least one alternative (therapeutic, mechanistic, tool, etc.)

Be strict:
- Only answer YES if the system/behavioral component is the main driver of impact
- If the study is primarily about a drug/device effect → answer NO
- If unsure → answer NO
